The Disciplines of MMA

Mixed Martial Arts demands mastery across multiple realms. Click on the view buttons to explore the elite champions who dominate using these core combat assets.

1. Boxing

The sweet science of punching, head movement, and footwork. Boxing isolates hand strikes to maximize speed, accuracy, and rotational power.

MMA Advantage: Controls the distance using the jab, coordinates complex pocket exchanges, and sets up precise knockout trajectories.

2. Muay Thai

Known as the "Art of Eight Limbs," Muay Thai incorporates punches, kicks, knees, and elbows alongside an advanced standing clinch system.

MMA Advantage: Devastating leg kicks that dismantle an opponent's mobility, and heavy elbow strikes inside the close-quarters clinch.

3. Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ)

A ground-fighting art centered around positional dominance, leverage, chokes, and joint locks to force a submission without striking.

MMA Advantage: Provides the ultimate defense when taken down and weaponizes the ground floor through guard-passes and joint submissions.

4. Wrestling

Focused on explosive takedowns, control, and physical leverage to force opponents to the canvas and dictate positional momentum.

MMA Advantage: Serves as the ultimate "dictator of the fight." Strong wrestlers decide whether a bout stays standing or goes to the ground.

5. Judo

A grappling system dedicated to shifting an opponent's balance to execute high-impact body throws, sweeps, and trips.

MMA Advantage: Exceptional inside the clinch against the cage wall, converting defensive body locks into sudden, devastating throws.

6. Karate

Emphasizes lightning-fast, linear blitzes, precise point striking, counter-attacks, and wide, low-center stances.

MMA Advantage: Elite management of range. Karate-style fighters use blitzing in-and-out footwork to strike without being counter-hit or wrestled.
